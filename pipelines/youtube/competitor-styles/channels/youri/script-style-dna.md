## Identity snapshot

- **Format:** faceless screen-recording tutorials about AI tools, faceless-YouTube automation, and make-money-online strategy. Voice-over over live software demos; no talking head.
- **Audience:** aspiring creators / side-hustlers who want results fast and cheap ("without needing years of editing or design experience", 0l07b_Aj1Mc).
- **Energy:** fast (median 206 WPM), dense, relentlessly confident but "honest-broker" — praises AND criticizes every tool.
- **POV:** first-person practitioner-who-has-done-it ("I have grown four different YouTube channels to over 100,000 subscribers each", 74JEr6bTszw). Heavy second-person "you".
- **Business model:** affiliate-funnel-first — most videos soft-sell a tool (OpenArt / VidIQ / Hostinger / Pixlz / Hera). Style is cloned; the funnel and its specific claims are NOT (see Do-not list).

## Hook formulas

Hooks run **~20–45 seconds / ~70–160 words** and always end on an explicit promise before the first demo. Five recurring patterns:

**1. Aspirational proof → "cheap AI tool did it" (product-demo videos).** Near-verbatim template reused across videos.
- "all of these YouTube videos have millions of views and surprisingly their thumbnails were not made by an expensive thumbnail designer but by a cheap and easy to use AI thumbnail generator that allows you to make thumbnails like this this or this in Just 2 minutes" (9Xifo4iMb6A)
- "All of these motion graphics look insanely professional, but surprisingly they were not made by a high-level video editor, but by an easy to use AI motion graphic generator that lets you make graphics like this, this, or this with just one prompt" (0l07b_Aj1Mc)

**2. "I tested them all" authority + filter promise (roundups/rankings).**
- "I tested every single free AI video generator on the market to see if you can actually create highquality video without spending a dollar… after filtering through the garbage, I narrowed it down to a couple of generators that are completely free and actually usable" (pgxFACkE7Fo)
- "I've tested every major tool that's come out recently, and I've seen where they shine and where they completely fall apart. So, in this video, I'm going to show you the AI video tools that actually deliver" (tQ84XYcP-nA)

**3. Result / income proof up front (money & strategy videos).**
- "this faceless YouTube channel has made over $84,000 in the last 6 months the best part is these videos are easily made with AI in this full guide I will show you how" (pXPu3hAR2zE)
- "this video has 280,000 views and made $960 this video also has 280,000 views and made $4,600 that's five times more money with the same amount of views" (mx_DuM13ZVo)

**4. Rock-bottom → transformation (before/after) with authority stack.**
- "28 views that's how many views my videos used to get when I did not understand YouTube keyword research now that I do I've got hundred[s] of videos ranking number one" (taI_CktpwOU)
- "I have found a cheat code to make money on YouTube from day one of starting your channel. This means you can start a channel now and tomorrow be making money" (eAUBU2hzsBA)

**5. Pain-mirror / reassurance ("imagine you… you're not behind") — older & newest bookends.**
- "I want you to imagine this you put in hours and hours of work into our YouTube shorts and they get stuck at just 20 [views]… well let me tell you this you are focusing on the wrong things" (H0ocsEiwEOM, 2022)
- "If it feels like everyone's already ahead of you on AI agents, they're really not because this is so new that hardly anyone has figured out how to build one properly yet. So, in the next 12 minutes, I'm going to get you completely set up" (FeKkztzebak, 2026)

**Hook tail = an explicit multi-part promise** (almost always 2–3 items) e.g. "I'm going to show you the AI video tools that actually deliver. The best way to use them without wasting hours… and the one platform that ties everything together" (tQ84XYcP-nA). Roundup hooks often close with the signature **"Let's break it down."** / "let's dive in".

## Structure map

The channel runs seven repeatable skeletons. Beat percentages are of total runtime.

**A. Multi-tool ROUNDUP ("Best X in 2026")** — tQ84XYcP-nA, pgxFACkE7Fo
- Hook + promise (0–6%) → per-tool beat ×3–5 (75%), each = name-drop → what it's for → live demo (often text-to-X then image-to-X) → verbatim prompt read aloud → honest pros+cons → "who it's for" verdict → open-loop transition to next tool → sponsor/aggregator pitch reframed as the solution (12%) → CTA (3%).

**B. RANKED "worst-to-best" / TIER LIST** — 12Qm1KfgRic (S/A/B/C/F), pgxFACkE7Fo
- Same as A but with an explicit ranking device and a **same-prompt methodology**: "the prompt I'm using for all of them goes like this…" (12Qm1KfgRic). Rankings payoff at the end: "The best three overall are Sora 2, Cling 2.5, and Google Vo 3.1."

**C. HEAD-TO-HEAD w/ SCORING RUBRIC** — OHODMrUZlpo
- Hook (0–6%) → declare categories + scoring ("All the tools in our rating card will receive a score from one to five stars") → category × tool matrix with running "report card" (85%) → final scoreboard + winner (= affiliate) → CTA.

**D. SINGLE-TOOL FULL TUTORIAL** — VJKGwXWtxMY (Invideo), FeKkztzebak (Hermes)
- Capability-demo hook + multi-part promise (0–8%) → account/setup → each feature as a sequential beat (70%) → "my personal opinion on pricing" verdict → **final-result reveal** ("here's the final video that I generated", VJKGwXWtxMY) → CTA.

**E. SINGLE-PROJECT "N-step workflow" COURSE** — oEzIrS7cM3M (5-step cartoon), taI_CktpwOU (3-step)
- Hook + "reduced everything to an effective five-step workflow" (0–6%) → Step 1…N, each numbered and each usually a different tool, building ONE artifact end-to-end (88%) → final artifact reveal + aggregator pitch → CTA.

**F. MONEY / STRATEGY explainer** — 74JEr6bTszw (KVS), eAUBU2hzsBA (HPCP), mx_DuM13ZVo (CPM), H0ocsEiwEOM (shorts)
- Hook w/ income/reframe (0–8%) → coin a NAMED framework/acronym → proof screenshots ("six pages of commissions of $250 coming in almost every single day", eAUBU2hzsBA) → walk the framework beat-by-beat (often embeds the reused VidIQ keyword module) (70%) → "5 lessons I learned" wrap → self-loop CTA to another of his videos.

**G. QUESTION-TITLE investigation** — nPXguQobCIs
- Everyday-scenario hook → "in today's video i'm gonna explain if it's possible to… or not" → investigate on-screen → clear yes/no verdict → soft CTA. (Older, casual register.)

## Pacing & rhythm

- **WPM:** mean **209**, median **206**, range **180–239** across all 27 (words ÷ duration from frontmatter). Fast, near-zero dead air — screen-demo narration is continuous.
- **Runtime:** median **~12 min**; roundups/courses run 15–25 min; the flagship keyword course is 60 min (taI_CktpwOU); older strategy shorts ~6 min (H0ocsEiwEOM 6:15).
- **Sentence length:** long, run-on, comma-spliced spoken sentences — clauses stacked with "so / and / now / then / which means". Example (one breath): "so what I'm going to do is I'm going to make sure that the first 30 seconds are perfect have a lot of different clips so that it flows nicely and the viewers are not going to click away" (pXPu3hAR2zE).
- **Questions:** frequent rhetorical questions used as pivots and to voice the viewer's objection — "Now what?" (tQ84XYcP-nA), "why does a video like this get so many views let's take a look" (pXPu3hAR2zE), "is replicating other people's thumbnails even allowed" (9Xifo4iMb6A).
- **Repetition devices:** triple-tap demonstratives "like this this and this" / "this, this, or this" (74JEr6bTszw, 0l07b_Aj1Mc); escalation triads "the next video the next video the next video the next video" (H0ocsEiwEOM); "again and again and again" (74JEr6bTszw); "very very" intensifier ("very very accurately", 9Xifo4iMb6A).
- **Emphasis via absolutes:** "absolutely insane", "ridiculously better", "that's insane", "honestly", "genuinely", "trust me".

## Language fingerprint

- **Recurring phrases / verbal tics (verbatim):** "Let's break it down" / "let's dive in" (openers); "with that said" / "with that being said" (transitions); "here's the thing" / "here's what I'd do"; "trust me"; "honestly"; "for example" (before nearly every demo); "the best part is…"; "which is insane" / "which is crazy"; "I'll leave a link [for you] in the description down below"; "so that you can follow along"; "and there we go" / "and here's the result" (after every generation); "you get the point"; "which brings us to step number [n]".
- **Vocabulary level:** conversational, low-jargon; explains any acronym immediately ("CPM stands for cost per meal [mille]", mx_DuM13ZVo). Coins its own memorable acronyms: **KVS** (74JEr6bTszw), **HPCP** (eAUBU2hzsBA), "three content layers" (pXPu3hAR2zE).
- **Contractions / slang:** contractions throughout ("you're", "I've", "it's", "doesn't"). Mild slang: "the garbage", "glorified free trials", "burning cash", "a pain in the ass" (pXPu3hAR2zE). Older videos add "guys" ("so in short guys", nPXguQobCIs) — largely dropped post-2023.
- **Direct address:** constant "you"; frequent imperative coaching ("Feel free to take a screenshot, by the way", eAUBU2hzsBA; "I want you to scroll through this list", 74JEr6bTszw).
- **Transparency register:** volunteers downsides in-line ("I want to be totally transparent… some of these results took two generations", 0l07b_Aj1Mc).
- **Words/registers they NEVER use:** profanity (kept to "a pain in the ass" at worst); hedging academic qualifiers ("arguably", "it could be argued"); corporate-buzzword filler ("leverage synergies", "at the end of the day" is rare); doom/hype clickbait it can't back with an on-screen demo. Newest scripts (FeKkztzebak) avoid "guys" and casual filler entirely — measured and clean.

## Transitions & retention devices

- **Open-loop question pivot between items:** "But what if you want something way different?… That's exactly where Pix[verse] comes in." / "But while Pixverse gives you those visuals, what if you need something more grounded?… That's where Cling comes in." (tQ84XYcP-nA).
- **Pattern-interrupt "wait until you see this one":** "even though all of that stuff works wait until you see this one" (H0ocsEiwEOM); "before I show you the sexy stuff that's coming up" (taI_CktpwOU).
- **Forward-reference open loops:** "I'll show you how to do that later on in this video" / "more about that later as well" (VJKGwXWtxMY); "I've actually got a little prompting guide section coming up later" (9Xifo4iMb6A).
- **Objection-handling as a beat:** pre-empts the viewer's doubt and answers it — "you might wonder is replicating other people's thumbnails even allowed… I completely understand but let me show you why in my opinion this is not the case" (9Xifo4iMb6A).
- **Reused modular teaching blocks:** the VidIQ keyword walkthrough (matching terms → open ranking videos → views-per-hour → Ctrl+F matching-keyword title → rapidtags.io tags) appears near-verbatim across 74JEr6bTszw, eAUBU2hzsBA, taI_CktpwOU.
- **Named-framework callback:** the acronym (KVS/HPCP) is set up early and each letter/step is a labeled beat, so the viewer always knows where they are.

## CTA style

- **Soft affiliate, seeded early + closed late.** Early seed: "if you want to know how I ax all of the AI models in just one tool, it's because I use open art. I'll leave a link to it down below so that you can follow along" (tQ84XYcP-nA). Late pitch reframes the tool as the SOLUTION to a problem the video built.
- **Promo codes / deals read verbatim:** "use my promo code Yuri to get an exclusive 60% discount on the annual plan" (74JEr6bTszw, Incogni); "applying the code yvh at checkout… 150 thumbnails for just $1" (9Xifo4iMb6A); "just $1 for 30 days" (VidIQ, across keyword videos).
- **Trust disclaimer when applicable:** "I am not sponsored by VidIQ. However, I will leave a link to the best VidIQ deal on the internet" (eAUBU2hzsBA); "this video is not sponsored by viq however I asked them if they can arrange a special deal for my subscribers" (taI_CktpwOU).
- **Value-reciprocity ask (free-course videos):** "in this video I will not sell you any course or coaching… in return please leave a like on this video And subscribe that's all I'm going to ask for" (taI_CktpwOU).
- **End-screen self-loop is the default close** — points to ANOTHER of his videos, not just "subscribe": "I've actually made a free 1hour video which you can watch right here I'll see you there" (74JEr6bTszw); "click the video on the screen or the link in the description and I will see you there" (mx_DuM13ZVo). Sign-off: "Thanks for watching and I'll see you in the next one."
- **Aspirational one-liner closers:** "because your next viral video is just one thumbnail away" (9Xifo4iMb6A); "you were never really behind. You were just one setup away and now that's done" (FeKkztzebak).

## Do-not list (a clone must NOT do these)

- **Never copy his specific factual claims, income screenshots, view counts, CPM numbers, promo codes, or sponsor reads** (e.g. "$84,000 in the last 6 months", code "yvh", "$1 for 30 days"). Clone the *structure* of proof, not the numbers.
- **No talking-head / face-cam** — this is a faceless screen-recording channel. No "as you can see me here".
- **No dead air or slow builds** — never let narration lag the screen action; keep ~200 WPM density.
- **Don't hype a tool without an on-screen demo of it** — every claim is shown live, including its failures.
- **Don't hide the downsides** — the trust lever IS naming what a tool gets wrong ("a product selling video doesn't have to be all positive. In fact it's even better if you also mention the downsides", eAUBU2hzsBA). A clone that only praises breaks voice.
- **No profanity beyond the mildest** ("a pain in the ass" is the ceiling); no doom-clickbait it can't back up.
- **Don't end on a bare "subscribe"** — always self-loop to another specific video or reframe the tool as the solution.
- **Don't stack unexplained jargon** — coin/define a simple acronym instead.
- **Avoid the older "guys" register** in new scripts — post-2023 voice is cleaner and more measured.
