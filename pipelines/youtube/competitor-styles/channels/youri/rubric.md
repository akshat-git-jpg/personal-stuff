## Checks (binary pass/fail)

1. **Hook uses one of the 5 DNA hook formulas** and lands in ~20–45s / ~70–160 words. (DNA: Hook formulas.) e.g. aspirational-proof, "I tested them all", income-proof, rock-bottom→transformation, or pain-mirror/reassurance.
2. **Hook ends on an explicit 2–3 part promise** before any demo, ideally closing on "Let's break it down." / "let's dive in". (DNA: Hook formulas — "Hook tail".)
3. **Structure matches one of the 7 DNA skeletons** (roundup / tier-ranking / scored head-to-head / single-tool tutorial / N-step course / money-framework / question-investigation) and holds the beat order. (DNA: Structure map.)
4. **Every tool/claim is shown via a live on-screen demo**, including a verbatim prompt or command read aloud where relevant. (DNA: Structure map D/E; Do-not: "Don't hype a tool without an on-screen demo".)
5. **Honest critique present** — the draft names at least one downside/failure of the tool or method, not only upsides. (DNA: Language fingerprint / Do-not: "Don't hide the downsides".)
6. **Pacing is dense (~200–210 WPM feel):** long comma-spliced spoken sentences, no slow build, no dead-air narration. (DNA: Pacing & rhythm.)
7. **Second-person "you" throughout + imperative coaching** ("I want you to…", "Feel free to…"). (DNA: Language fingerprint.)
8. **At least 2 signature verbal tics used verbatim** from the DNA set ("with that said", "the best part is", "and there we go / here's the result", "I'll leave a link in the description down below", "trust me", "honestly", "for example"). (DNA: Language fingerprint.)
9. **A named framework/acronym or numbered structure is coined** when the video teaches a method (e.g. KVS/HPCP/"three content layers"/"five-step workflow"), and any acronym is defined on first use. (DNA: Language fingerprint; Structure E/F.)
10. **Transitions use open-loop pivots / pattern-interrupts** between items ("But what if you want…? That's exactly where X comes in", "wait until you see this one", "which brings us to step N"). (DNA: Transitions & retention devices.)
11. **An objection is pre-empted and answered** where the topic invites doubt ("you might wonder… I completely understand but let me show you why"). (DNA: Transitions & retention devices.)
12. **CTA is a soft affiliate seeded early + reframed as the solution late, OR a value-reciprocity like/sub ask** — never a bare "subscribe". (DNA: CTA style.)
13. **Close self-loops to another specific video or an aspirational one-liner**, ending "…I'll see you in the next one." (DNA: CTA style.)
14. **Title (if generated) fits a ★ DNA title pattern** — "Best [X] in 2026 (qualifier)", "How to [outcome] with AI (Full Guide)", "[Tool] - Full Tutorial 2026", "FORGET X!", or reassurance "Master X in N Minutes" — with a front-loaded SEO-exact keyword. (DNA: Title patterns.)
15. **No Do-not violations:** no invented income/stat numbers or copied promo codes/sponsor reads, no talking-head phrasing, no profanity beyond mild, no unexplained jargon, no "guys" register in a new-style script. (DNA: Do-not list.)
