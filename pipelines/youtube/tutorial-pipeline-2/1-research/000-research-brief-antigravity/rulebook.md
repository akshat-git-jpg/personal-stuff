# Rulebook 000 · research-brief (paste into Antigravity)

You are preparing a research brief for a YouTube tutorial video. The person recording the
video will follow this brief section by section. They are good at demonstrating software on
screen but they will NOT do their own research, so the brief has to carry all of it.

TOPIC: <fill in: tool/product + angle, e.g. "Cursor AI - full beginner walkthrough">

Research the topic on the official docs, the product changelog, and 2-3 community sources
(Reddit, HN, the product's Discord/forum). Then write `<base>.brief.md` with exactly these
sections:

## Audience and promise
Two sentences. Who this video is for and what they can do after watching it.

## Demo sections (in recording order)
5-9 numbered sections. Each section is one thing shown on screen, written as an instruction
to the recorder ("Open X, click Y, show what happens when Z"). Order them so each builds on
the previous. The recorder will record these IN THIS ORDER with a 2-second pause between
sections, so make each section self-contained.

## Facts to state (verified)
Bullet list of concrete claims the recorder should say out loud: pricing, limits, version
numbers, dates. Every fact needs the source URL next to it. If a fact could not be verified,
leave it out. Do not include a fact you inferred.

## Pitfalls and FAQs
The 3-5 things beginners get wrong or ask about, pulled from the community sources. Each
one: the problem in one line, the fix in one line.

## Terminology
Any product names, acronyms, or version strings the voiceover will need, each with a plain
phonetic spelling (this feeds the pipeline's pronunciation map).

Keep the whole brief under 700 words. No filler, no intro paragraph, no conclusion. The
recorder reads this once and records.
