## HeyGen

### identity_notes
- [[04:26]] note: An AI avatar tool that does a bit of everything with high quality results.
- [[04:54]] note: Features an option to start from a video to clone yourself exactly by shooting a video in a professional environment.
- [[05:48]] note: Includes a library with a ton of different voices.
- [[07:28]] note: Has an Avatar 4 option for absolute highest quality movements.

### pricing_claims
- [[15:22]] plan: subscription plans -- price: unspecified -- detail: says unlimited videos and the videos can be up to 30 minutes long
- [[15:47]] plan: creator tool -- price: unspecified -- detail: For the highest quality avatar generation, you actually get 15 minutes a month using the creator tool.
- [[20:14]] plan: $29 a month plan -- price: $29 a month -- detail: you can get up to 15 minutes before needing to buy more credits

### strengths
- [[04:26]] strength: Most well-rounded AI avatar tool with all necessary features and really high quality.
- [[08:18]] strength: High quality video output with pretty natural face and hand movements, and animated PowerPoint slides changing in the background.
- [[09:12]] strength: Allows adding captions and making them bold to make videos easier to watch.
- [[09:31]] strength: Allows adding minor custom movements and hand gestures to characters.
- [[14:05]] strength: A great tool for most use cases.
- [[15:08]] strength: A great option for most use cases of generating realistic AI avatars.
- [[15:59]] strength: The results and quality are worth the expensive price.

### weaknesses
- [[06:15]] weakness: Some voices (like 'a lay') sound off and do not fit a corporate professional setting, sounding more like a storybook narrator.
- [[09:00]] weakness: The extra expressive motion setting can make the character look overly excited and inappropriate for corporate videos.
- [[09:53]] weakness: Custom motions are subtle and cannot perform dramatic actions like taking off a jacket.
- [[14:13]] weakness: There is a slight, off-putting stutter in between words.
- [[14:39]] weakness: Freezes the avatar's body between sentences, making it look uncanny and noticeable.
- [[14:52]] weakness: Issues with background elements, such as volcano smoke proofing out and shrinking back rather than rising realistically.
- [[15:28]] weakness: The unlimited video feature does not apply to Avatar 4, which is the highest quality tool.
- [[15:56]] weakness: It is a bit of an expensive tool.

### quirks
- [[08:33]] quirk: The avatar has super super expressive eyebrows when talking.
- [[09:45]] quirk: Adding custom motion costs a bit more.

### demos
- [[04:38]] demo: Uploaded a realistic photo generated with Nano Banana Pro, named the avatar Janice, and selected a voice named Jessica Anne Bogart.
- [[06:55]] demo: Entered a corporate training script, set Avatar 4, and toggled the advanced settings for expressive movements.
- [[07:53]] demo: Generated two versions of the avatar: one with default motion and one with extra expressive motions.
- [[09:12]] demo: Generated a video of the avatar with bold captions and custom hand gestures.
- [[13:47]] demo: Animated the custom Janice avatar talking while holding the Amazon book cover.
- [[14:07]] demo: Created a podcast style avatar.
- [[14:47]] demo: Created a cinematic style explorer avatar with a volcano background.

### comparisons
- [[17:54]] comparison: design offers smoother, more fluid character body movements than Hey Gen.
- [[18:00]] comparison: design does not have the weird starting and stopping in between sentences seen in Hey Gen.
- [[18:14]] comparison: Hey Gen has many more features than design, which is more bare bones.
- [[18:19]] comparison: Hey Gen allows adding subtitles and prompting for specific movements, which design does not.
- [[18:27]] comparison: design animates backgrounds better than Hey Gen, such as smoke rising consistently from a volcano.
- [[19:50]] comparison: Hey Gen allows generating videos up to 30 minutes long, whereas design only allows audio files up to 5 minutes.
- [[20:00]] comparison: Hey Gen is less expensive than design, offering 15 minutes on its $29/month plan compared to design's roughly 5 minutes and 30 seconds on a $25/month plan.

### verdict
- [[04:26]] verdict: Considered the most well-rounded AI avatar tool because it does a bit of everything and has high quality results that are worth the price.
