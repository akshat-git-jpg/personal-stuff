## HeyGen

### identity_notes
- [[00:00]] text: An AI avatar tool that generates unlimited AI videos and images that look, talk, and gesture like the user.
- [[00:18]] text: A tool that automates content workflow without the need to shoot videos, set up lighting, or be on camera.
- [[00:46]] text: Designed for business owners who want to post consistently but hate being on camera or don't have time to shoot.

### pricing_claims
- [[01:31]] plan: free trial -- price: free -- detail: when you sign up for Hijen, you actually get one free instant avatar and one free credit as well, which lets you create a 60-second video.
- [[01:43]] plan: $30 month plan -- price: $30 month -- detail: I personally use a $30 month plan. With that, you get unlimited videos per month, but you do not get unlimited avatar 4 generation. Now, the videos can actually be up to 5 minutes long, so you're not limited to just 60 seconds per video. You get 300 instant avatars, which means you can film yourself in 300 different outfits or even 300 different backgrounds.
- [[02:13]] plan: Avatar 4 -- price: $15 a month -- detail: Avatar 4. This is an upgrade that costs $15 a month for 5 to 10 minutes of video generation.
- [[09:43]] plan: Avatar 4 -- price: extra $15 a month -- detail: Avatar 4 cost an extra $15 a month

### strengths
- [[01:08]] claim: Features options for instant avatar, photo avatar, template, AI script, and editor.
- [[01:54]] claim: Gives 300 instant avatars, allowing users to film in 300 outfits or backgrounds to keep content fresh and make it harder to detect as AI.
- [[02:17]] claim: Avatar 4 upgrade provides much better lip sync, more realistic body language, and smoother hand movements.
- [[05:03]] claim: Allows uploading more footage over time to fine-tune and keep training the AI avatar.
- [[06:08]] claim: Consent validation only takes about 20 seconds.
- [[06:13]] claim: Avatar processing takes only about 2 to 3 minutes.
- [[07:17]] claim: Seamlessly syncs the avatar's mouth and gestures to uploaded audio files.
- [[07:23]] claim: Video rendering/generation takes about a minute or two.
- [[09:39]] claim: Fine-tuning gives access to higher resolution.

### weaknesses
- [[03:54]] claim: The AI struggles with hands, meaning users need to keep complicated movements to a minimum.
- [[08:29]] claim: Limited strictly to talking head videos; users cannot perform crazy visual hooks, jump around, or show hands doing complex actions.
- [[08:48]] claim: Showing the avatar for more than 6 seconds at a time causes viewers to notice something is off.
- [[10:05]] claim: The photo clone option looks fake and makes it obvious that an avatar is being used.

### quirks
- [[03:40]] claim: Requires pausing between sentences with the mouth closed so the AI can see the resting face; otherwise, lip sync is off.
- [[03:49]] claim: Requires keeping hands below the chest zone.
- [[03:59]] claim: Requires training footage to have no cuts/stitches, no non-stop talking, no changing positions, no background noise, no shadows or overexposures on the face, and no looking away.
- [[05:44]] claim: Requires users to record a quick consent video reading a legal authorization script to prevent deep fakes.

### verdict
- [[02:24]] text: Highly recommends upgrading to Avatar 4 if serious about creating content for clients or personal brands.
- [[04:44]] text: Recommends against using webcams for recording training footage due to low quality, unless only testing it out.
