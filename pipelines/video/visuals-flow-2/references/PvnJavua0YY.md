## Mode structure — when he shows what (all 3 videos, owner question)

Modes and their share of runtime (tile-count estimate from 10s-interval
overview sheets):

- **Full-screen talking head ("avatar"): ~25-30%.** Fires ONLY as bridges:
  (a) the trust/setup beat after the cold-open (~20-60s in), (b) 10-30s
  between sections — announce the next round, react to a result, deliver an
  opinion too nuanced for a chip, (c) the final recommendation block.
  Never during evidence: the moment a claim needs proof he cuts away.
  TH camera is static — no punch-ins; energy comes from cutting AWAY to
  content, not from zooming the host. Entered by hard cut; exited by green
  wipe/flash.
- **Content base (footage / UI / phone windows): ~50-60%.** Everything
  evidence-shaped. The presenter stays present as the **corner avatar bubble**
  (top-right circle, green ring) — his answer to "avatar presence" during
  content. Effects riding this mode: verdict chips, score pills, prompt
  panels, progressive tables, model-name chips; UI screencasts always get the
  slow push-in; result clips play near-full-screen (v1) or in floating
  windows (v2/v3).
- **Pure motion-graphics canvas: ~15-20%.** No host at all: kinetic-sentence
  interstitials (spoken-sync yellow keywords), icon slates, chip stacks,
  stack diagrams, step banners, scoreboard. Used when the WORDS are the
  evidence (lists, claims, numbers, section starts). Typical hold 1.5-4s.
- The cycle is fast and regular: TH bridge (10-30s) → wipe → content run
  (30-90s, chips/pills accumulating) → wipe → canvas beat (2-4s) → …
  Cuts ~10-14/min in content runs; a wipe/flash lands every 15-40s.

## Rule deltas vs the ranking format

1. Tutorial sections are numbered (#1/#2/#3 banners) instead of icon round
   slates.
2. Chip checklists describe PROCESS STEPS (imperative sentences) not criteria.
3. Phone-vertical windows replace 16:9 result windows (UGC context).
4. Same transition economy: green wipe/flash INTO content and INTO canvas
   beats; hard cuts back to host.
