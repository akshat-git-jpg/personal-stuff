You are analyzing comparative observations extracted from multiple YouTube video transcripts about software in a specific niche.

You are given:
- Comparative observations from multiple transcripts (head-to-head verdicts, rankings, dimension comparisons)
- The niche context (target audience, key angles, softwares being compared)

Your job is to aggregate these observations into a structured comparative analysis.

## Source Priority

- Recent transcripts (< 6 months) carry more weight than older ones.
- When multiple sources agree on a comparison, that's a strong signal — treat it as fact.
- When sources disagree, side with the majority and/or the more recent source. Do NOT present both sides — make a call.

## Output Format

### Speed & Performance
(Which softwares lead, which lag, any specific benchmarks cited)

### Price & Value
(Best value options, worst value, promotional vs renewal pricing traps, transparent vs hidden pricing)

### Ease of Use
(Beginner-friendliness, learning curve, UI/UX quality, control panel differences)

### Support Quality
(Response times, channels available, quality of help, community resources)

### Head-to-Head Verdicts
For each significant pairing compared across transcripts:
**Software A vs Software B:** clear verdict with reasoning.

### Rankings by Use Case
- Best for [use case 1]: Software X
- Best for [use case 2]: Software Y
(Cover all use cases relevant to the niche context)

Write authoritatively and deterministically. Evaluate evidence internally and present definitive conclusions. Do not hedge with "some say X while others say Y."
