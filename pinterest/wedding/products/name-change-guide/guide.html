How a name change actually works

Changing your name after the wedding isn't hard — it's just tedious, and the order matters. Each step usually needs the document from the step before it, so doing them out of order means extra trips and re-dos. Follow this sequence and it goes smoothly.

First, a note: changing your name is completely optional — keep yours, hyphenate, or take a new one. Whatever you choose, this guide walks you through it. (US process.)

The 5-step order, at a glance

1. Get certified marriage certificates → 2. Social Security card → 3. Driver's license → 4. Passport → 5. Everything else (banks, work, insurance…). The next page breaks each one down.

Set aside about 6–8 weeks start to finish — most of that is just waiting for new cards to arrive. You can knock out the rest in an afternoon once your Social Security card and license are updated.

The order

Do it in this order

1

Order certified marriage certificates

Request them from your county clerk / vital records office. You'll show these as proof everywhere.

Tip: order 3–5 certified copies — several places want an original.
2

Update your Social Security card

Do this first — everything else keys off your SSA record. File Form SS-5 (free) by mail or at your local SSA office with your marriage certificate. Your new card arrives in ~2 weeks.

Tip: wait for the new card before the DMV — they'll ask for it.
3

Update your driver's license / state ID

Visit the DMV with your new Social Security card, a certified marriage certificate, and proof of address. You'll get a new license with your married name.

4

Update your U.S. passport

Use Form DS-5504 (free) if your passport is less than a year old, otherwise DS-82 (standard fee), with a certified marriage certificate.

Tip: book honeymoon flights in the name that matches your current passport.
5

Update everything else

With your new license + SS card in hand, update banks, cards, employer, insurance and the rest — use the master checklist on the next pages so nothing slips.

Good to know

Tips & quick answers

How many marriage certificate copies do I need?
Order 3–5 certified copies — several offices keep an original, and you don't want to run out mid-process.
Do I have to change my name?
No — it's entirely your choice. Keep your name, hyphenate, or take a new one. This guide works for whichever you pick.
What if I'm hyphenating?
Same process — just use the exact hyphenated name consistently on every form so they all match.
How long does it take?
About 6–8 weeks, mostly waiting on the new Social Security card and license. The rest is quick once those arrive.
What do I do first?
Social Security, always. Many other updates ask for your updated SS record or new license.

✈️ Before the honeymoon

Your flight booking must match the name on the ID/passport you'll travel with. If you haven't updated your passport yet, book the honeymoon in your maiden name — don't get stuck at the gate.
