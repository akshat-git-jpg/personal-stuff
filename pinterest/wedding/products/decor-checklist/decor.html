How to use this checklist

Decor is where the little things hide — the signs, the candles, the cake knife no one remembers until the day-of. This checklist walks your wedding zone by zone so every detail is accounted for, decided, and assigned.

  • Walk it in order — ceremony → cocktail hour → reception → extras. Picture a guest moving through your day.
  • Tick only what applies. No venue needs everything — skip freely, add your own in the blank rows on each zone.
  • Decide rent / DIY / buy for each item as you go (rentals get returned; DIY needs storage + time).
  • Count quantities. Centerpieces = number of tables; place cards = number of guests. Write the number next to it.
  • Assign setup & teardown. Who places it, who takes it home? Decor left behind = lost deposits.
  • Group by box. Pack each zone's decor in its own labeled bin so setup is grab-and-go, not a treasure hunt.
  • Check the rules. Many venues ban open flame, confetti, or anything stuck to walls — confirm before you buy.
  • Build a return pile. Keep one bin for rentals and one for "goes home with us" so nothing gets boxed by mistake.
R = rent  ·  D = DIY  ·  B = buy Use the note line under each zone for qty · who sets up · who tears down.

One pro move

Snap a photo of every finished area at the rehearsal so your team can recreate it exactly on the day — and you can finally stop holding it all in your head. Keep a shared album so every helper is working from the same picture.
