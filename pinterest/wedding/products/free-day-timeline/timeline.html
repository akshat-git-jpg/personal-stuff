How to build a timeline that flows

A good wedding-day timeline is the difference between a calm, joyful day and a rushed one. The trick is simple: start from your ceremony time, then work backward for getting ready and forward for the reception. Here's how the pros do it:

  • Anchor to the ceremony. Lock that time first; everything else builds around it.
  • Pad every block by ~15 minutes. Hair runs long, traffic happens — buffer saves the day.
  • Do a "first look." It lets you knock out couple + wedding-party photos before the ceremony, so you actually enjoy your cocktail hour.
  • Chase the golden hour. Slip away for couple portraits ~30–45 min before sunset — the light is unreal.
  • Feed your people. Build in time for the wedding party (and you!) to eat.
  • Share it with everyone. Give the final timeline to every vendor and the whole party.

The next page is a real, worked example for a 4:00 PM ceremony — the most common start time. Shift every block earlier or later to match your own ceremony hour.
