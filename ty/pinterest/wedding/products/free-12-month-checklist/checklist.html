Hey, bride-to-be 👋

First — congratulations! You're engaged, and somewhere between the happy tears and the "wait, where do I even start?" panic, you found this. Take a breath. You've got a whole year (or however long you've got), and you do not have to figure it all out today.

I built this checklist to break the entire wedding down into small, doable steps, spread across the months ahead — so instead of one terrifying to-do list, you just handle a little at a time. Work top to bottom, tick things off as you go, and let the rest wait its turn.

How to use this checklist

  • The timeframes are guidelines, not rules — planning in 6 months? Just start at the top and move faster.
  • Tick the box once something's booked or done, not just researched.
  • Print it and stick it on the fridge, or keep the PDF on your phone — whatever keeps you looking at it.
  • Money and guest count come first on purpose: almost every other decision depends on them.
  • Each page has a "notes & my own to-dos" block — jot down the bits that are unique to your day.
  • Don't panic if a box stays empty for a while. You're allowed to do this at your own pace.

One last thing before you dive in: this is your wedding, not a competition or a checklist for anyone else. Skip what doesn't fit, add what does, and lean on the people who offered to help — most of them genuinely meant it. The goal isn't a flawless event; it's marrying your person surrounded by the people you love.

You've got this — I'm right here with you. xo, your Bride Bestie
