Get started

How to use your Vault

  1. Start with the Ultimate Wedding Planner. It's your home base — fill in your details and vision, then work the 12-month checklist from the top.
  2. Open the Budget Tracker alongside it. Pop in your total budget and add costs as you book — it keeps you on track in real time. (Open the link, then File → Make a copy.)
  3. Grab the wedges when you hit them: the Decor Checklist as you style the day, the Timeline as the date nears, and the Name-Change Guide for after the wedding.
  4. Overwhelmed? Switch to the ADHD-Friendly Checklist — one tiny step at a time, no shame.
  5. Print what helps (US Letter), keep the rest on your phone or tablet. Use it your way.

Your Google Sheet: the Budget Tracker opens as a "make a copy" link — it drops a private, editable copy into your own Google Drive. The original stays untouched, so you can always grab a fresh one.

By stage

How the pieces fit together

  1. Just engaged. Open the 12-Month Checklist and the Budget Tracker first — they tell you what's next and what you can spend, so every later decision has a frame around it.
  2. Deep in planning. Live in the Ultimate Planner. Vision, guests, vendors and the running to-do list all sit here, and the budget updates alongside as you book.
  3. The final stretch. Bring in the Day Timeline and the Decor Checklist so the actual day is mapped hour by hour and zone by zone — nothing left to memory.
  4. After the wedding. The Name-Change Guide takes it from there with the exact order to update everything, so the post-wedding admin stays as calm as the rest of it.
