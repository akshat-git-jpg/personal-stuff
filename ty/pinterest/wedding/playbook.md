# Wedding niche playbook

The recipe card for `pinterest-make-post`. Brand/colors/fonts live in `config.json`; this is voice,
hooks, formats, keywords, and do/don'ts. (Always also obey `../PINTEREST-PRINCIPLES.md`.)

## Who we're talking to
Newly-engaged brides (and some grooms) who just started planning, feel overwhelmed by everything
there is to do, and are on Pinterest *right now* looking for a way to get organized. They're spending
real money on the wedding and will happily pay a little to feel in control.

## Offer / funnel
Direct to **Gumroad** (no email list yet). Every pin drives to **"The Ultimate Wedding Planner"** —
a printable PDF bundle (checklist + 12-month timeline + budget breakdown + guest/RSVP + vendor
questions), ~$19–29. CTA is value-forward: "Get the full printable planner →".

## Voice
Warm, reassuring, organized big-sister energy. "Your dream day, without the overwhelm." Calm and
competent — you've got a plan, follow it step by step. Never preachy, never pushy. Inclusive of all
weddings (budgets, sizes, couples).

## Proven formats (from research — replicate these)
- **The checklist** (anchor): "The Ultimate Wedding Planning Checklist", "The Wedding Checklist You'll
  Actually Use". → `listicle` / `free-guide`.
- **Budget breakdown** with a specific number: "How to Budget a $10,000 Wedding", "Where Your Wedding
  Budget Actually Goes". → `listicle` / `recipe-card` (allocation chips).
- **Day-of timeline**: "12-Month Wedding Planning Timeline", "Wedding Day Timeline for a 2PM Ceremony".
  → `weekly-grid` (months/hours as cells).
- **Decor / guest / name-change** mini-guides. → `listicle`.

## Hooks that win
- "Where do I even start?" → "The only wedding checklist you need."
- Specific numbers: "$10k wedding", "12 months", "10 steps".
- Relatable curiosity: "The checklist you'll *actually* use", "What no one tells you about wedding budgets".
- Payoff + ease: "Plan your whole wedding without the overwhelm — done-for-you printable."

## Visual theme (LOCKED — backed by analysis of the 9 top wedding pins, 2026-06-02)
> **Accent = blush/dusty-rose + gold** (chosen 2026-06-03). Color analysis of the top 60 pins: ~82%
> light base, and the feed is warm-dominant (45% peach/beige, 13% blush). Blush = "closest to wedding
> feel" but it BLENDS in — so we stand out via **execution** (oversized serif, max whitespace,
> high-contrast on-image text, curiosity copy), not color. (Cooler sage/blue would pop more if we ever
> want a pattern-interrupt test.)
**Light, elegant, airy — NOT dark.** 8 of 9 top wedding pins are light (cream/ivory/white) with dark
text. Dark backgrounds underperform here: the product is a *printable* (light = looks printable),
the emotion is dreamy/soft, and dark text on cream is most legible at thumbnail size. So we go light
— but engineer ATTENTION through contrast, not darkness:

- **Base:** warm ivory (`#FBF5F2`). Soft, premium, prints clean.
- **Headline:** oversized elegant serif (Playfair Display) in **deep charcoal (`#34302B`)** —
  this high-contrast headline is the scroll-stopper. Keyword goes IN the headline (on-image text).
- **One accent for pop:** a single **dusty-rose / blush (`#C58A86`) or gold (`#C9A24B`) band/strip**
  behind the title or as the CTA strip. One accent only — don't rainbow it.
- **Show the product:** a printed checklist/planner on a pretty surface — soft blush florals, neutral
  linen, soft natural light. It must read as "a real thing I get."
- **Airy beats crammed:** the #1 pin was the MOST minimal (giant serif + lots of cream space). Dense
  text-walls rank lower. Out-class competitors by being the cleanest, most premium pin in the feed.
- **Mobile check:** big, high-contrast text; readable when shrunk to a thumbnail.

**Pattern-interrupt test variant (run alongside, let data decide):** occasionally make ONE bright
variant — soft illustration + a saturated color band for the headline (à la the "12 Things Brides
Regret" pin) — and compare its saves/clicks to the elegant ones. Measure attention, don't guess.
Never go full-dark for wedding.

## Keywords (reuse consistently across pin/board/description — alignment)
wedding planning checklist · ultimate/complete wedding planner · printable · bride / bridal ·
wedding budget breakdown · wedding day timeline · order of events · ceremony to reception ·
wedding planning for beginners · where to start · name change checklist

## Do / Don't
- DO keep it reassuring and step-by-step; lead with the payoff; bake the keyword into the image.
- DO drive every pin to the Gumroad planner with a clear CTA.
- DON'T name specific vendors/brands or make price guarantees ("venues always cost $X").
- DON'T keyword-stuff; write natural sentences.
- DON'T imply only big/expensive weddings — include budget-conscious and small weddings.
