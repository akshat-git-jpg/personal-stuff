# Keto niche playbook

The "brain" the make-post skill reads so every pin sounds and looks like keto. Edit freely.

## Voice
Warm, encouraging, no-nonsense. Talks to a busy person who wants results without willpower
struggles. Confident, never preachy or medical.

## Hooks that sell on Pinterest (keto)
- Carb counts: "under 10g carbs", "net 4g carbs"
- Speed / ease: "15-minute", "5-ingredient", "no-cook", "sheet-pan", "meal-prep once"
- Specific outcomes: "stay full till lunch", "beat the 3pm crash"
- Numbers + roundups: "7 ... ", "12 ... " (listicles perform)
- "Free" / "printable" for the lead magnet

## Example winning titles (imitate the shape, not the words)
- "7 High-Protein Keto Breakfasts (Under 10g Carbs)"
- "15-Minute Keto Dinners for Busy Weeknights"
- "The Lazy Keto Meal Plan (Free Printable)"
- "5-Ingredient Keto Lunches You'll Actually Crave"

## Image vibe
- Bright, natural light. Single hero dish, close-up or overhead flat-lay.
- Appetizing keto palette: eggs, bacon, avocado, salmon, leafy greens, cheese, berries.
- Avoid the "sad diet food" look — food should look indulgent and real.
- Clean bold typography over the image or a deep-green/gold color block.

## Keywords to weave into descriptions
keto recipes, low carb, keto diet, keto meal plan, ketogenic, high protein, keto breakfast,
keto dinner, easy keto, beginner keto, keto for weight loss

## Do
- Keep claims lifestyle-level ("stay full", "simple", "beginner-friendly").
- Always end the description with a soft CTA toward the guide.

## Don't
- No medical or rapid-weight-loss claims ("lose 10 lbs in a week", "cures X").
- No "doctor recommended" / health-authority phrasing.
- Don't overcrowd the image with text.
