# Structure

Target length: **15–20 minutes spoken** (can go longer if the niche justifies it).
Spoken pace: ~140 wpm.
Word target: **2,100–2,800 words**.

Default format: **Tier-list comparison**. Use head-to-head or category-winner only when the KB clearly calls for it (see §10).

---

## 1. The four tiers

Every tool in the KB lands in exactly one tier. Decide tier placement from the KB's verdicts and rankings — not from intuition.

| Tier | Label | What it means | Time allocation |
|------|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| 🔴 | **Eliminated** | Don't use this. Specific, consequence-first reason. | 2–3 min total |
| 🟡 | **Situational** | Works, but only in one narrow scenario. | 1–2 min total |
| 🟢 | **Worth Considering** | Solid, didn't make the top. | 1–2 min total |
| 🏆 | **Winners** | What I'd actually recommend. 1–4 tools. | 4–6 min total |

**Winners get the most time. Eliminated gets the most drama.** Situational and Worth Considering are the shortest — they're setup for the Winners payoff.

---

## 2. Section blueprint

Full script follows this order. Word counts are targets per section for a ~17-min video.

| # | Section | Target length | Target words |
|---|---------|---------------|--------------|
| 1 | Hook | 25–45s | 60–110 |
| 2 | Intro (categories + who this is for) | 45–75s | 105–175 |
| 3 | Eliminated (per tool) | 20–35s each | 50–90 per tool |
| 4 | Situational (per tool) | 25–45s each | 60–110 per tool |
| 5 | Worth Considering (per tool) | 25–45s each | 60–110 per tool |
| 6 | Winners (per tool) | 60–110s each | 150–280 per tool |
| 7 | Final Summary | 25–45s | 60–110 |
| 8 | Outro + CTA | 20–40s | 50–100 |

Winner #1 (the top pick) can stretch slightly longer. Winners #2–4 tighten as the list goes on.

---

## 3. The Hook (25–45s)

Three ingredients, in any order, but all three must appear:

1. **Confirm the click.** First sentence proves the viewer is in the right place. Match the search query's exact mental state.
2. **Introduce tension.** One sharp contradiction, consequence, or promise the viewer hasn't heard. Examples: "n8n is completely free. So why are so many people accidentally spending $80/month to run it?"
3. **Plant the payoff.** "I went through all 15 of the most popular ways to host n8n — and I sorted every single one so you don't have to guess." Tease the structure without spoiling rankings.

**Never in the hook:** a bio, a table of contents, a "subscribe" ask, the word "video."

---

## 4. The Intro (45–75s)

Three jobs, in this order:

1. **Explain the framework.** Name the four tiers and what each one means in one line.
2. **Tell the viewer who this is for.** Quick picture of the workflows / use cases the tools serve. Makes the next 15 minutes feel relevant.
3. **Cold-open the first section.** Transition line that sets up Eliminated: "Let's start with the ones to avoid — and more importantly, why."

No bio. No "subscribe for more." No "this is going to be a long one so grab a coffee."

---

## 5. Eliminated tier (2–3 min total)

One tool at a time. Each tool follows the same mini-structure:

1. **Name + one-line acknowledgement of what it does well.** (Gives credibility — you're not just dunking.)
2. **The specific flaw, explained mechanically.** What breaks. Show the consequence at 11pm when it fails.
3. **Short verdict.** "Don't do it." / "Skip it." / "Move on." / "Eliminated."

20–35s per tool. Six eliminated tools at the long end = ~3 min.

Do not turn this section into a roast. Every tool was real to the person who built it. The tone is "I'm warning you" not "I'm mocking them."

---

## 6. Situational tier (1–2 min total)

One tool at a time. Each:

1. **"If you're [specific scenario], this works."** Lead with the scenario, not the tool.
2. **The catch that makes it situational.** What has to be true for the fit to work.
3. **Who it's actually right for.** One-line qualifier.

25–45s per tool. No verdict line — the qualifier line does that work.

---

## 7. Worth Considering tier (1–2 min total)

Same structure as Situational, but framed positively:

1. **Name + short descriptor of what kind of user recommends it.**
2. **The specific strength.** Price, performance, specific feature.
3. **The one friction point** that kept it out of Winners.

25–45s per tool. These are real options — don't damn them with faint praise.

---

## 8. Winners tier (4–6 min total)

The payoff. More time per tool. Warmer language.

Each winner:

1. **Intro line — who this one is for.** "The first winner — and the one I'd recommend to most people — is [X]." Or: "The second winner is for a specific type of person: [description]. That's [X]."
2. **The price/spec anchor.** One concrete number the viewer can hold onto.
3. **The 2–3 specific things it does better than the rest.** Each one tied back to a real workflow the viewer will actually do.
4. **The honest tradeoff.** Every winner has one. Naming it builds trust.
5. **CTA line with the link callout.** Picks get a warmer push (see voice.md §11).

Winner #1 (top pick) is the warmest and longest (90–120s). Later winners tighten to 60–90s.

If there's a "hidden gem" / non-obvious combo (like Coolify + Hostinger in the n8n example), it goes last as Winner #N.

---

## 9. Final Summary (25–45s)

One line per winner. One line grouping Worth Considering. One line for Situational. One line reminding the viewer which Eliminated tools to avoid by name.

> **Hostinger** — easiest setup, best price. Right for most people.
> **Railway** — zero setup, live in five minutes. You pay more for that.
> **DigitalOcean** — reliable, scalable, best for serious business use.
> **Coolify on Hostinger** — the best value combination on this list.

This section exists to leave the viewer with a card they can screenshot. Make it screenshottable.

---

## 10. Outro + CTA (20–40s)

Three beats:

1. **Confidence close.** "You now know which platforms are worth your time, which ones will quietly let you down, and exactly which one fits where you are right now."
2. **Link callout for all covered tools, warmest for tier-list picks.** Emphasize that picks have discount codes if applicable.
3. **Comment hook + sign-off.** Question that makes the viewer self-identify and type. Then: "Thanks for watching. I'll see you in the next one."

No "like and subscribe." No bonus pitch. Out.

---

## 11. When NOT to use the 4-tier structure

Default is 4-tier. Switch only if the KB tells you to:

- **Head-to-head (2 tools)** — when the niche is effectively a rivalry (X vs Y) and the KB compares them directly. Structure: Hook → Both introduced → 4-5 criteria compared → Deciding factor → Who the loser is actually good for → CTA.
- **Category winner (3+ personas)** — when the KB's comparative-insights recommend different tools for different user types and there's no single "winner." Structure: Hook → "The answer depends on who you are" → 3-4 personas defined → One pick per persona → Summary → CTA.

Never mix formats within a script. Pick one and commit.

---

## 12. Output format — script only

The output is a **spoken script**. No B-roll, music, camera, energy, or production directives. The script is what the presenter reads aloud.

File layout:

```markdown
# <Title — matches a real search query>

~<N>–<N+1> minutes spoken.

---

## HOOK

Spoken paragraph.

Spoken paragraph.

---

## INTRO

Spoken paragraph.

---

## ELIMINATED: <Tool>

Spoken paragraph.

Verdict line.

---

(...continue per tool, per tier...)

---

## WINNER #1 — <Tool>

Spoken paragraphs.

CTA line.

---

## FINAL SUMMARY

Spoken paragraphs / bullet recap.

---

## OUTRO

Spoken paragraphs.

Sign-off line.
```

Rules:

- **Section headers only** — no per-section timestamps in the body. State a total runtime estimate once at the top.
- **Plain prose paragraphs** under each header. One idea per paragraph. Paragraph breaks are the speaker's natural pause cues.
- **No spoken-line markers** (🎙️ etc). Everything under a header is read aloud.
- **No B-roll cues, music notes, energy directions, or camera blocks.** If the user later wants a production layout, that's a separate pass — never mixed into the script file.
- **Link notes are in the spoken text only** ("link's in the description"). No `📎 LINK NOTE` blocks for editors.
- **Reference the example scripts** in `example-script/` — both are now plain-script format and should be matched.

---

## 13. Word-count sanity check

Before handing over, count the spoken words. A 17-min video at 140 wpm is ~2,380 words. Outside the 2,000–3,000 range means something is wrong — usually a winner is too long or the intro is bloated.
